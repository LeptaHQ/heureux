## **Q1**

### **Passage**

```
Maman,
Je fais mes devoirs de mathématiques chez Louise.
Je rentre à 21 heures. Bises et à ce soir.
Patrick

(Mom,
I'm doing my math homework at Louise's.
I'll be home at 9 PM. Kisses and see you tonight.
Patrick)
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Qu'est-ce que Patrick fait chez Louise ? | What is Patrick doing at Louise's? |
| A | Il dort | He sleeps |
| **B** | **Il travaille** | **He works/studies** |
| C | Il joue | He plays |
| D | Il mange | He eats |

### **Correct Answer: B -- Il travaille**

"Je fais mes devoirs de mathématiques" (I'm doing my math homework) = he is working/studying. "Travaille" here means academic work, not a job.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- Il dort.** He's doing homework, not sleeping. He'll be home at 9 PM -- not staying overnight.

**C -- Il joue.** He's doing homework (devoirs), not playing.

**D -- Il mange.** No mention of food or a meal. The purpose of the visit is homework.

"Le contrôle aboutit à pervertir l'action" (assessment perverts the action) + students substitute real reading with summaries and ready-made answers to pass exams = assessment disrupts genuine learning.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- Améliorer les méthodes.** The opposite: assessment leads to "abominations" like summaries replacing real reading. It degrades methods.

**B -- Indispensable pour le monde professionnel.** The author's argument is AGAINST the value of assessment/diplomas, calling them "phantom guarantees."

**C -- Certifier les compétences réelles.** The author says diplomas certify "une science momentanée" (momentary knowledge) -- a phantom, not real skills.
