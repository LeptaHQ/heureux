## **Q1**

### **Passage**

```
Chers Amis,
Nous sommes heureux de vous annoncer l'arrivée d'un nouveau membre dans notre famille.
Il se nomme Adrien et a vu le jour le 1er septembre au matin.
Bien à vous
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | De quel événement s'agit-il ? | What event is this about? |
| A | D'un anniversaire | A birthday |
| B | D'un décès | A death |
| C | D'un mariage | A wedding |
| **D** | **D'une naissance** | **A birth** |

### **Correct Answer: D -- D'une naissance**

The passage announces "l'arrivée d'un nouveau membre dans notre famille" (the arrival of a new member in our family) and says "il se nomme Adrien et a vu le jour le 1er septembre" (his name is Adrien and he was born on September 1st). The expression "a vu le jour" literally means "saw the light of day," which is a standard French idiom for being born. The family states they are "heureux" (happy), and the overall structure -- short, warm, addressed to friends -- follows the format of a birth announcement. The key strategic signal is that the passage introduces someone who did not previously exist, distinguishing it from all other event types.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- D'un anniversaire.** A birthday celebrates someone who is already alive and known. The passage describes a "nouveau membre" (new member) who "a vu le jour" (was born), meaning this person is being introduced for the first time. A birthday announcement would reference an age or a celebration, not the arrival of someone new. The vocabulary of novelty and arrival eliminates this option.

**B -- D'un décès.** A death notice would carry a somber, mournful tone. Here, the family says they are "heureux" (happy) and use the word "arrivée" (arrival), which implies someone coming into the world, not leaving it. The joyful tone is the clearest disqualifier for this option.

**C -- D'un mariage.** A marriage announcement would reference a ceremony, a couple, a union, or a wedding date. Instead, the passage gives a single person's name, a birth date, and describes a new family member. There is no mention of a partner, a wedding, or any celebration of a union.

## **Q2**

### **Passage**

```
Désolée, je ne viens pas aux cours de français et je ne peux pas travailler
à la bibliothèque avec toi aujourd'hui. Je suis malade et je reste à la maison.
A bientôt,
Sandra
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Qu'est-ce que Sandra fait ? | What is Sandra doing? |
| A | Elle étudie son français | She is studying her French |
| B | Elle invite son ami Rémi | She is inviting her friend Rémi |
| **C** | **Elle reste chez elle** | **She is staying at home** |
| D | Elle va à la bibliothèque | She is going to the library |

### **Correct Answer: C -- Elle reste chez elle**

Sandra explicitly states "je reste à la maison" (I'm staying at home) because she is sick ("je suis malade"). The expression "chez elle" in the answer choice means "at her place/home," which is a direct synonym for "à la maison." The strategic approach here is straightforward: look for the answer that directly restates what the passage says. Sandra cancels two activities (French class and library work) and gives one positive statement about what she IS doing -- staying home. That positive statement is the answer.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- Elle étudie son français.** Sandra says the exact opposite: "je ne viens pas aux cours de français" (I am not coming to French class). She is explicitly cancelling her French studies. When a passage uses a negative construction ("ne...pas") about an activity, any answer that presents that activity as happening is automatically wrong.

**B -- Elle invite son ami Rémi.** There is no mention of anyone named Rémi anywhere in the passage, and Sandra is cancelling plans, not making new ones. This option introduces information that does not exist in the text. When an answer choice contains a proper noun or detail absent from the passage, it is almost always a distractor.

**D -- Elle va à la bibliothèque.** Sandra writes "je ne peux pas travailler à la bibliothèque" (I cannot work at the library). She explicitly states she cannot go. This is another case where the passage directly negates the answer choice.

## **Q4**

### **Passage**

```
SAMEDI
Marthe: 12h-13h Cours de natation
Djamel: 11h-13h Revision avec Claire

DIMANCHE
Marthe: Course avec Claire
Djamel: 14h-18h Répétition
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Que fait Marthe le samedi à 12 heures ? | What does Marthe do on Saturday at noon? |
| A | Elle chante | She sings |
| B | Elle court | She runs |
| C | Elle étudie | She studies |
| **D** | **Elle nage** | **She swims** |

### **Correct Answer: D -- Elle nage**

The schedule shows "Marthe: 12h-13h Cours de natation" on Saturday. "Natation" means swimming. At noon on Saturday, Marthe has a swimming lesson, so she swims ("elle nage"). The strategic approach for schedule-based questions is to isolate two variables: the person (Marthe) and the time (Saturday at 12h). Only one cell in the schedule matches both: Saturday, Marthe, 12h-13h, Cours de natation. Every other option corresponds to a different person, a different day, or a different activity.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- Elle chante.** There is no singing activity for Marthe. Djamel has "Répétition" (rehearsal) on Sunday, which could imply singing or performing, but that belongs to a different person on a different day. This is a classic cross-contamination trap: information from another person's schedule is offered as if it belonged to Marthe.

**B -- Elle court.** Marthe does run ("Course avec Claire"), but that activity is listed under SUNDAY, not Saturday. The question specifically asks about Saturday. This is a day-swap distractor designed to catch students who identify the right person and activity but fail to verify the day.

**C -- Elle étudie.** Studying/revision ("Revision avec Claire") is Djamel's activity on Saturday, not Marthe's. This is a person-swap distractor: the right day, but the wrong person.

## **Q5**

### **Passage**

```
Une société de production RECHERCHE
Pour faire de la FIGURATION dans un film sur le thème parents/enfants,
de 7 jeunes mères confrontées à l'éducation de leurs enfants.
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Que recherche la société de production ? | What is the production company looking for? |
| A | De jeunes enfants | Young children |
| B | Des femmes et des enfants | Women and children |
| **C** | **De jeunes mamans** | **Young mothers** |
| D | Des parents avec leurs enfants | Parents with their children |

### **Correct Answer: C -- De jeunes mamans**

The ad specifically says the company is looking for "7 jeunes mères" (7 young mothers). "Jeunes mamans" is a direct synonym for "jeunes mères." The strategic skill here is recognizing paraphrases: the correct answer will almost never use the exact same words as the passage, but instead a synonym or equivalent expression. "Mères" becomes "mamans" -- same meaning, different word.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**A -- De jeunes enfants.** The ad seeks MOTHERS, not children. The thème involves parents/children, but the casting call is specifically for mothers. This distractor exploits the fact that children are thematically present, hoping you will confuse the theme of the film with who is being recruited.

**B -- Des femmes et des enfants.** Only mothers are sought. While the film's thème is about parents and children, the casting specifically calls for mothers alone. This option is too broad -- it adds "children" as people being recruited, when only mothers are needed. In TCF questions, an answer that is partially correct but adds extra information not supported by the passage is wrong.

**D -- Des parents avec leurs enfants.** The ad says "jeunes mères" (young mothers), not "parents" in general. "Parents" would include fathers, but the ad exclusively targets mothers. This distractor generalizes where the passage is specific.

## **Q6**

### **Passage**

```
A Héloïse,
Héloïse, quand tu viendras chez moi, pense à prendre des vêtements chauds.
À la montagne, en hiver, il fait très froid. Je t'ai envoyé les horaires du bus par SMS.
Appelle-moi ce soir quand tu rentres du travail.
Vivement les vacances!
Bises, Sam
```

### **Prompt & Answers**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Que doit faire Héloïse ? | What should Héloïse do? |
| **A** | **Bien choisir ses vêtements** | **Choose her clothes carefully** |
| B | Chercher les horaires du bus | Look for bus schedules |
| C | Envoyer un courriel a Sam | Send an email to Sam |
| D | Trouver une location de vacances | Find a vacation rental |

### **Correct Answer: A -- Bien choisir ses vêtements**

Sam tells Héloïse "pense à prendre des vêtements chauds" (remember to bring warm clothes) because it's cold in the mountains in winter. She needs to choose her clothes carefully -- specifically, warm ones. The strategic reasoning: the prompt asks what Héloïse MUST DO ("doit faire"). Look for an imperative or instruction directed at her. "Pense a prendre des vêtements chauds" is a direct instruction, and the answer "bien choisir ses vêtements" is its paraphrase.

### **Why the others are wrong**

**B -- Chercher les horaires du bus.** Sam already SENT her the bus schedules by SMS ("Je t'ai envoyé les horaires du bus par SMS"). The past tense "ai envoyé" means this task is already done. She does not need to look for them. This distractor turns a completed action into a pending task.

**C -- Envoyer un courriel a Sam.** Sam asks her to CALL ("Appelle-moi"), not email. Additionally, calling Sam back is a secondary request, while the main instruction -- the one given more textual weight and explanation -- is about bringing warm clothes. Even if "call" were an option, "choose clothes" would still be the primary answer because it receives more emphasis in the passage.

**D -- Trouver une location de vacances.** The accommodation is already arranged -- Héloïse is coming to Sam's place ("chez moi"). There is no rental search needed. This distractor invents a task that the passage has already resolved.

## **Q13**

|  | French | English |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prompt** | Selon l'article, que va faire l'équipage du voilier la Boudeuse ? | According to the article, what will the crew of the sailing ship la Boudeuse do? |
| A | Former des scientifiques à la prévention des risques écologiques | Train scientists in ecological risk prevention |

### **Correct Answer: C -- Observer l'écosystème dans une region de grands fleuves**

The ship heads to "l'Amérique du sud et ses grands fleuves" (South America and its great rivers) for "études scientifiques et humaines concernant la biosphère" (scientific and human studies concerning the biosphere). **A -- Former des scientifiques à la prévention des risques écologiques.** The mission is to CONDUCT studies, not to TRAIN scientists. "Former" (to train) implies an educational mission, but the passage describes research ("études scientifiques"), not pedagogy. There is no teaching component in the mission letter.

**B -- Innover dans les moyens de lutter contre les pollutions fluviales.** The mission concerns studying climate change, biodiversity, and sustainable development -- observational and analytical work. It does not mention innovating anti-pollution methods or fighting river pollution specifically. Innovation and pollution-fighting are action-oriented, whereas the mission is study-oriented.

**D -- Représenter le gouvernement français lors d'une rencontre.** While the mission letter comes from a minister ("signée du ministre d'État"), the crew's task is scientific study, not diplomatic representation. The government authorized the mission, but the crew is not serving as diplomats or attending a meeting.
